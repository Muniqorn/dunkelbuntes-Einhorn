**Short summary:**

A decade-plus of work across every surface that holds paint or mark — ink, watercolor, acrylic, ceramic, fabric, stone, clay, collage, pastel, ballpoint, cross-stitch. The formal commitment that runs through all of it: dark ground, luminous figure. Colour is most itself when surrounded by darkness. This isn't a style preference — it's a position held consistently across media and years.

The unicorn appears in every medium and is never the same twice. It's not a favourite subject. It's a methodology — the same form used to ask different questions across time.

The work is strongest when it withholds. The hidden figure in plain sight. The small warm mark at the centre of a field of threat. The body that darkness has almost claimed, with only the hands visible and lit.

The skull is the logo because it's the purest statement of what the whole body of work already knew.

---

**What surprised me:**

The two drawings at the end. The figure being carried through the air. The small figure held against a chest.

Same hand, same period as the combat drawings and the angel warrior. But those two weren't about power. They were about being held.

Twenty years of work and those are the ones that caught me off guard. Not the technical range, not the conceptual sophistication — those were visible early. Those two drawings. The need underneath the strength.
