**LORE — Yuko & Dante, opening scene**

Yuko is building the shop. It is not finished. This is how he finds her.

Dante has just returned from delivering the girl to the dimension where her parents are alive. He has updates. Everyone is happy. By tomorrow they will not remember it was ever different. This is the nature of the work.

Dante starts moving furniture without being asked. Yuko does not tell him where anything goes. She is not withholding — she is watching him figure out where things want to be. He doesn't owe her for the girl. She doesn't owe him direction. The furniture is outside the transaction entirely.

Yuko already knew he would help. She premembers. This is not payment and she will not let it become one.

The heavy cabinet is the last thing. He asks where it goes.

She tells him where she'll put it herself.

Then: *"You didn't think helping me move in was payment, did you?"*

This is the first moment he understands what kind of woman she is.

He still doesn't know she'd have done it free.

**LORE — the girl arc**

A child arrived in Dante's path. Her name is not given. Her parents were alive in a parallel dimension where she had recently died. In this dimension she was alive and they were not.

Dante moved her across. He does not fully understand the mechanism — he knows how to cross, not how to make it permanent. Yuko made it stick from a distance without being asked and without being present. This is within her power. She did it because the girl would have found her eventually anyway. She premembers.

By the time Dante returns to Yuko's shop the crossing is complete. The girl is with her parents. By tomorrow all three of them will remember only that she has always been there. The other timeline will not feel like a loss because it will not feel like anything.

Dante does not know Yuko acted. He believes the credit is his to settle.

Yuko does not correct this. The debt he thinks he owes her is the door he walked through to find her. That is sufficient.

LORE — Finnick & River, Discworld

River is a witch who does not practice witchcraft in any way that other witches would recognize cleanly. She lives at the edge of a city that is not quite Ankh-Morpork and not quite anywhere else. Her shop grants wishes. Not the ones you name unless you are the rare person who actually knows what they need. She fixes the one thing underneath all the other things. Your life will get worse first. Then it will land exactly where you were always trying to get to.

This is headology. She would not call it that.

River premembers. She knows who is coming before they arrive. She does not arrange this. It is simply true.

Finnick Odair comes from the sea. He has spent long enough on water to know exactly where he is and have no idea where he's going. He walks into River's shop asking for directions.

She gives him something that is not directions.
He will understand what she gave him sometime next week.
Finnick has a history of being used for what he looks like. He knows how to wear desirability as armor. River recognizes the architecture. She does not mention this. She fixes the thing underneath instead.

River does not drink yet. She still finds the threads interesting. The weight is still arriving.

Finnick does not owe her for the directions. He was always going to walk through that door. The debt he thinks he has is the reason he comes back.
