WHAT ""TO DIE"" DOESN'T COVER

Etruscan actually has two separate words in the death cluster: ""lein-"" (? to die) and ""lup-"" (to die — possibly cognate with Finnish loppua, ""to end, to terminate, to come to an end""). ""Lupu"" separately means ""dead, deceased.""

If lein simply meant ""to die"" in the same register as lup-, why two words? Languages don't redundantly double concepts without distinction. Lup- likely covers the terminal state — the ending, the termination. Lein- is doing something else in the same semantic field.

Not die. Follow. Go after. Accompany.
